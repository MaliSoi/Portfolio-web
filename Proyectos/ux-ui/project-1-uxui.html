Portfolio personal | Malala Soifer

Brief

I needed a portfolio where I could bring together my two profiles, since I recently transitioned into the digital world and wanted to channel all my experience and potential in one single space.

Timeline: 3 meses
Service: UX/UI - web designer
Role: Principal Designer
Tools: Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, Visual Studio Code, HTML, CSS, JS

The Challenge

The main challenge was to merge both worlds —my background in textile design and my new path in UX/UI— into one clear, engaging, easy-to-read place that wouldn’t overwhelm, and that felt like me.

The Process

It was a really enjoyable process, especially because this time the project was about me. But I have to admit that at times it felt a bit overwhelming —when the focus is on yourself, applying everything you've learned can feel tricky. It’s often easier when the portfolio belongs to someone else. Still, I took on the challenge with excitement.

Galería

The Solution

I found a way to combine my UX/UI knowledge with what I’ve been learning about front-end and web development. The goal was simple: to build a clean and functional portfolio that could reflect both sides of me as a designer.

Outcomes & Learnings

I learned that working for yourself is not always easy, which is why I turned to the people around me for feedback and support during the tricky moments. I also discovered how rewarding it is to see things take shape. And above all, I’m proud to have built this portfolio from scratch.
