Swap Now | App

Brief

Swap Now is an app designed to give clothing a second life by making it easier for users to exchange garments in a seamless and sustainable way. Through user research, wireframing, and prototyping, I developed an intuitive experience that encourages circular fashion while ensuring a smooth swapping process. The design focuses on usability, trust, and accessibility, making sustainable fashion more engaging and practical for everyone.

Timeline: 3 meses
Service: UX/UI - web designer
Role: Principal Designer
Tools: Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator.

The Challenge

Explain the problem you were trying to solve or the opportunity you identified. What were the constraints? Who were the users? What were their needs? Research Insights: Explain the problems you identified through your research. You can mention the methods used (interviews, surveys, competitive analysis). Visuals: If you have user personas, empathy maps, or charts/graphs of research findings (e.g., "70% of users struggled with X"), this is a good place to include them as images or small diagrams alongside the text. They don't have to be UI designs, but rather evidence of your research.

The Process

Outline your design process. What research did you conduct? How did you approach the problem? What design decisions did you make along the way? User Flows: Show how users would navigate through the application to complete a key task. A flowchart is ideal here. Wireframes (Low and Mid-Fidelity): Present basic sketches or wireframes to demonstrate how information and interaction were structured before adding visual design. You can show a progression (e.g., hand-drawn sketch -> digital wireframe). Information Architecture (IA) Diagrams / Site Maps: If the project required it, a map of how the information is organized can be useful. Prototypes (Link): If you have an interactive prototype in Figma, InVision, etc., this is the perfect place to include a clear link ("View interactive prototype"). Design Decisions: Explain why you made certain decisions at this stage, based on your research or usability principles. Visuals: Include images of your user flows, wireframes, and other process artifacts here. These can be Figma screenshots or drawings.

The Solution

Describe your final design solution in detail. How does it address the initial challenge? What features did you implement and why? Here, in addition to the final UI screens (which you had already planned), you can make a direct connection between your research/wireframing process and the final solution. Suggested Content: How the final solution addresses the problems identified in the research. If you conducted usability testing, mention the results here and how they informed the final adjustments. How to integrate it visually: Don't overdo it: You don't need to show every single wireframe. Select the most representative ones or those that highlight a key point in your process. Accompany with text: Each image of a wireframe, flow, or research artifact should be accompanied by a clear explanation of what you are showing, why it's important, and what you learned from it.
